# MENTAL HEALTH QUIZ  - 10 Questions + Scoring
**Purpose:** Screen for emotional distress and route users to appropriate support resources  
**Scoring:** Each question = 0-3 points. Total range: 0-30.  
**Tone:** Hopeful, non-clinical, non-diagnostic ("based on your answers" language throughout)  
**Compliance:** AFSP guidelines, no diagnosis claims, crisis resources free to all

---

## SCORING GUIDE
```
0 = Not at all / Rarely  - 0 points
1 = A little / Sometimes  - 1 point  
2 = Quite a bit / Often  - 2 points
3 = Very much / Always  - 3 points
```

---

## THE 10 QUESTIONS

### Question 1: MOOD
**"Over the past two weeks, how would you describe your overall mood?"**

- ✅ I feel good more days than not (0 points)
- ✓ I feel okay, but flat or numb some days (1 point)
- ✗ I feel down or empty most days (2 points)
- ✗✗ I feel hopeless or dark almost all the time (3 points)

*Why this order:* Ranges from stable → numbness → depression → hopelessness. Captures the spectrum without using clinical language.

---

### Question 2: SLEEP
**"How have your sleep patterns been lately?"**

- ✅ I sleep pretty well and wake up rested (0 points)
- ✓ I have some nights of bad sleep, but it evens out (1 point)
- ✗ I'm sleeping way less than usual OR oversleeping most days (2 points)
- ✗✗ My sleep is completely disrupted  - I'm barely sleeping or sleeping all the time (3 points)

*Why this order:* Sleep is a biological marker of distress. Ranges from stable → occasional disruption → chronic disruption → severe disruption.

---

### Question 3: STRESS / OVERWHELM
**"How stressed or overwhelmed do you feel about your daily life right now?"**

- ✅ I'm managing my stress okay (0 points)
- ✓ I feel stressed, but I can usually handle it (1 point)
- ✗ I feel overwhelmed a lot and struggling to cope (2 points)
- ✗✗ I feel completely overwhelmed and can barely function (3 points)

*Why this order:* Direct measure of perceived coping. Ranges from adaptive coping → some strain → significant strain → acute crisis.

---

### Question 4: RELATIONSHIPS / CONNECTION
**"How connected do you feel to the people in your life?"**

- ✅ I have people I can talk to and I feel supported (0 points)
- ✓ I have some close people, but sometimes feel lonely (1 point)
- ✗ I feel pretty isolated and disconnected from others (2 points)
- ✗✗ I feel completely alone  - no one really knows or understands me (3 points)

*Why this order:* Social isolation is a major risk factor. Ranges from secure connection → occasional loneliness → chronic isolation → profound disconnection.

---

### Question 5: SELF-HARM / SUICIDAL IDEATION
**"In the past two weeks, have you had thoughts about hurting yourself or that you'd be better off dead?"**

- ✅ No, not at all (0 points)
- ✓ I've had a fleeting thought, but it didn't stick (1 point)
- ✗ I've had these thoughts regularly and they're hard to shake (2 points)
- ✗✗ I think about it constantly and have a plan (3 points)

*Why this order:* THE critical question. Direct, clear language. No euphemisms. Escalates from none → intrusive → recurrent → planned intent.  
**Note:** This single high score (2+) triggers immediate crisis resources regardless of total score.

---

### Question 6: SUBSTANCE USE
**"How have you been using alcohol, drugs, or other substances to cope?"**

- ✅ I don't use them, or I use them rarely and socially (0 points)
- ✓ I use them occasionally to unwind, pretty normal for me (1 point)
- ✗ I'm using more than usual to numb or escape (2 points)
- ✗✗ I'm dependent on substances to get through the day (3 points)

*Why this order:* Substance escalation tracks with distress. Ranges from non-use/social use → increased use → self-medication → dependence.

---

### Question 7: WORK / SCHOOL / RESPONSIBILITIES
**"How are you functioning at work, school, or with daily responsibilities?"**

- ✅ I'm managing well and keeping up (0 points)
- ✓ I'm keeping up, but it takes more effort than usual (1 point)
- ✗ I'm falling behind or avoiding responsibilities (2 points)
- ✗✗ I've basically stopped doing what I need to do (3 points)

*Why this order:* Functional impairment tracks with severity. Ranges from intact functioning → increased effort → impairment → collapse.

---

### Question 8: SOCIAL WITHDRAWAL
**"Have you been withdrawing from activities or people you normally enjoy?"**

- ✅ I'm doing things I enjoy and seeing people (0 points)
- ✓ I'm doing less than usual, but still getting out sometimes (1 point)
- ✗ I've withdrawn quite a bit from activities and people (2 points)
- ✗✗ I don't want to see anyone or do anything  - I'm isolating (3 points)

*Why this order:* Anhedonia (loss of pleasure) + withdrawal is a key depression marker. Ranges from engagement → reduced engagement → significant withdrawal → severe isolation.

---

### Question 9: ENERGY / MOTIVATION
**"How's your energy and motivation been?"**

- ✅ I have decent energy and feel motivated most days (0 points)
- ✓ Some days I'm tired, but I push through okay (1 point)
- ✗ I feel exhausted a lot, even after rest  - nothing feels worth it (2 points)
- ✗✗ I have no energy or motivation  - everything feels pointless (3 points)

*Why this order:* Fatigue + anhedonia are core depression/burnout markers + suicide risk indicators. Ranges from intact → situational fatigue → persistent exhaustion → profound anhedonia.

---

### Question 10: HOPELESSNESS
**"When you think about the future, how do you feel?"**

- ✅ I feel hopeful or at least accepting about what's ahead (0 points)
- ✓ I have some worry about the future, but mostly feel okay (1 point)
- ✗ I feel uncertain or pessimistic about the future (2 points)
- ✗✗ I feel hopeless  - I don't believe things will get better (3 points)

*Why this order:* Hopelessness is one of THE strongest suicide risk predictors. Ranges from hopeful → cautious → pessimistic → hopeless.

---

## SCORING LOGIC

### Score Calculation
Total all 10 responses (0-30 range)

```
0-10 points   → Low Risk (Green)
11-20 points  → Moderate Risk (Yellow)
21-25 points  → High Risk (Orange)
26-30 points  → Critical / Crisis (Red)
```

### OVERRIDE LOGIC (Mandatory)
**If Question 5 (Self-Harm) scored 3:** Jump directly to CRITICAL results page, regardless of total score.  
**If Question 5 scored 2:** Jump to HIGH RISK results page at minimum.

*Rationale:* Suicidal ideation with plan is not a spectrum issue  - it requires immediate crisis intervention regardless of overall functioning.

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (What NOT to do)

❌ Don't phrase as diagnostic: "Do you have depression?"  
✅ Do phrase as screening: "Do you feel down or empty?"

❌ Don't use clinical acronyms: "PTSD" "GAD" "MDD"  
✅ Do use lived language: "trauma," "anxious," "struggling"

❌ Don't ask about specific methods or plans for self-harm  
✅ Do ask about presence/recurrence of ideation + planning  

❌ Don't minimize: "Have you had ANY sad thoughts?"  
✅ Do validate: "Have you had thoughts that you'd be better off dead?"

❌ Don't ask in rapid-fire succession  
✅ Do space sensitive questions (Question 5 is mid-quiz, not at start)

## NON-GIMMICKY CTA RULES

- show the result immediately before asking for email or phone
- position contact capture as convenience, not access
- never say "unlock your results"
- never lead high-risk or critical users to a paid product first
- keep crisis resources visible whether or not someone opts in

Approved capture language:
- "Want a copy of your results?"
- "Want these next steps sent to you?"
- "Want these resources in one place?"

Avoid:
- "Unlock your score"
- "Enter your email to continue"
- "Get exclusive access"
