## TEMPLATE 1: Myth-Busting Hook (15-30 seconds)

**Purpose:** Break through the noise of mental health content by directly addressing why people don't do the thing they should do.

**Format:** Text overlay + talking head + close-up emphasis

---

### SCRIPT 1A: The "Plant the Idea" Myth

**[OPEN WITH TEXT ON SCREEN]**
"Most people believe asking about suicide plants the idea"

**[CUT TO YOU, LOOKING DIRECTLY AT CAMERA]**
"That's not what the research shows. And I've seen it firsthand in my work."

**[TEXT OVERLAY]**
"Studies consistently show: asking about suicide does NOT increase risk — it decreases it"

**[BACK TO YOU]**
"In fact, people who survive suicide attempts say the same thing: being asked made me feel *less* alone, not more at risk."

**[CLOSE-UP TEXT]**
"If you're not asking because you're scared you'll make it worse — you're already making it worse."

**[END TEXT]**
"Learn what to actually ask [LINK IN BIO]"

---

**Why this works:**
- Opens with a common belief (relatability)
- Contradicts it with research (credibility)
- Ends with a specific reason to click (CTA)
- Shareable premise (people save and repost this)

---

### SCRIPT 1B: The "I'm Not Trained" Myth

**[OPEN WITH TEXT]**
"You don't need training to ask someone if they're thinking about suicide"

**[YOU, DIRECT TO CAMERA]**
"You need courage. You need to care. Those are things you already have."

**[TEXT OVERLAY]**
"You're not being asked to be their therapist. You're being asked to be their friend."

**[CUT TO SPLIT SCREEN: You + text]**
"Your job: notice, ask, listen, help them connect to professional support"

"A professional's job: provide actual treatment"

**[BACK TO YOU]**
"Two different things. And guess which one you're already qualified for?"

**[END WITH]**
"Being a good friend IS the training"

## TEMPLATE 2: The Wrong/Right Script (30-45 seconds)

**Purpose:** Show the exact difference between what bombs and what works. Super shareable because people recognize themselves in the "wrong" version.

**Format:** Split screen or text comparison

---

### SCRIPT 2A: "Are You Okay?"

**[SPLIT SCREEN LEFT SIDE — "WRONG"]**
You: "Hey, are you okay?"
Them: "Yeah, I'm fine."
[END SCENE]

**[TEXT OVERLAY]**
"Conversation over. No help given."

**[SPLIT SCREEN RIGHT SIDE — "RIGHT"]**
You: "I've noticed you seem [different]. I care about you. Can I ask you something real?"

Them: *nods*

You: "Are you thinking about suicide?"

**[TEXT OVERLAY]**
"Real conversation starts"

**[YOU, DIRECT TO CAMERA]**
"The difference? You asked directly. No easy out. No escape route."

## POSTING STRATEGY FOR THESE SCRIPTS

### Content Mix (Suggested Weekly Rotation)

- **Monday:** Myth-busting (Template 1)
- **Wednesday:** Script comparison showing wrong vs. right (Template 2)  
- **Friday:** Quick tip or one of the 3 questions (Template 4)
- **Plus:** 2-3 Q&A responses per week (Template 5)
- **Bonus:** 1x personal credibility story per month (Template 3)

### Hashtag Strategy

- Large: #mentalhealth #suicideprevention #mentalhealthawareness #mentalhealthmatters
- Niche: #QPR #suicideintervention #crisisintervention #LCSW #socialwork
- Trending: vary weekly based on trends + mental health relevance

### Caption Strategy

Make captions **action-oriented** and **vulnerable:**

❌ "Check out this tip about suicide prevention"

✅ "I see SO many people trying to help but not knowing the actual words. Here's what the research says works."

### Link in Bio Strategy

- **Primary:** Link to lead magnet opt-in (get email list)
- **Secondary:** Link to full program once you have affiliate platform set up
- **Rotate:** Every few weeks, swap which link is primary so you can test conversion

---

## One Final Note

**These templates are starting points.** You'll find your own rhythm, your own way of saying things. The scripts above give you the structure — but your authenticity is what makes them work.

The combination of clinical credibility + personal story + genuine warmth = the formula that works on TikTok for this content.

Stay true to that, and the videos will resonate.
