# Ask Anyway — Cold DM Templates

Date: 2026-03-20
Status: Draft — personalize before sending

---

## Rules for Using These

**Goal of the cold DM:** Get a "tell me more" or "I'm interested." That's it. You are not pitching. You are opening a door.

- Keep it under 5 sentences
- Reference something specific about their content — do not send the same DM to 50 people
- Never say "affiliate opportunity" in the first message — it reads as spam
- Do not include a link, a price, or a commission number in the first message
- If they ask "what is it" — use the reply templates below, then send the one-pager

---

## Template A — First Responder / Fire / EMS / Police

> Hey [name] — your video on [specific topic, e.g., "that video about asking for help after a bad call"] hit me. I'm working on a mental health tool called Ask Anyway — a short guide series specifically for first responders on how to actually ask for support. I think your audience would connect with it. Would you want to hear more?

**Why it works:** Specific reference to their content → immediate credibility. "First responders" makes it relevant to their niche. Low-pressure close.

---

## Template B — Healthcare Workers (Nurses, Docs, Allied Health)

> Hey [name] — I watch your content regularly and the way you talk about burnout is something a lot of nurses/providers don't say out loud. I'm a [licensed clinical social worker / psych nurse practitioner — use your title] working on a program called Ask Anyway — short guides that help high-burnout folks actually learn to ask for support. I think it fits with what your audience already trusts you on. Would you be open to hearing about it?

**Why it works:** Your clinical title removes the spam read. You're not a random app — you're a clinician. "High-burnout folks" mirrors the language healthcare creators use.

---

## Template C — Military / Veteran / Gold Star

> Hey [name] — your content on [specific topic, e.g., "reintegration / grief / 'I'm fine' culture"] is something I don't see a lot of people talk about honestly. I'm building Ask Anyway, a guide for veterans and military families on how to ask for help without it feeling like a weakness. I'd love your take and think your audience would find it useful. Is this something you'd want to hear more about?

**Why it works:** "Without it feeling like a weakness" is culturally precise for this audience. "Your take" invites them in as a collaborator, not just a paid promoter.

---

## Template D — Mental Health Educator / Therapist / Counselor Creator

> Hey [name] — I'm a [LCSW / psych NP] and I've been following your channel for a while. I built a program called Ask Anyway — a structured guide series on help-seeking for people who intellectually get that they need support but freeze when it comes to actually doing it. I think it's something you could authentically recommend to your audience. If you're open to hearing the details, I'd love to share them.

**Why it works:** Peer-to-peer framing — clinician to clinician. "Intellectually get that they need support but freeze" is precise clinical language that resonates with MH educators. Authentic is the right word because therapist creators care about that.

---

## Template E — Parent / Educator / School Counselor

> Hey [name] — I've been watching how you talk about emotional support and [modeling help-seeking / talking to kids about hard things]. I made a program called Ask Anyway for adults at a breaking point who don't know how to ask for help — and I think a lot of the parents and educators in your audience are exactly that person. Would you want to see what it looks like?

**Why it works:** "Adults at a breaking point" resonates with parents and educators who are the caretakers and never ask for help themselves. Connects their community back to the product.

---

## Template F — Recovery / Addiction / Sobriety Creator

> Hey [name] — the way you talk about what it took to actually reach out [in early recovery / when it got bad] is real in a way most content isn't. I'm working on a program called Ask Anyway — short guides for people who are right at the edge of asking for help but can't get themselves to do it. I think your audience includes a lot of those people. Would you want to hear more?

**Why it works:** "Right at the edge of asking" describes exactly where recovery audience members live. The reference to their real content matters — do not skip it.

## If They Reply "What Is It?" — Reply Template

Use this in the reply before sending the one-pager. Keep it short.

> Ask Anyway is a set of short, practical guides — written for people who know they need support but don't know how to ask. The quiz at the start routes them to the right guide based on their situation. It's not therapy — it's the step before therapy, for the person who can't even get there yet.
> 
> There's a simple affiliate structure so if your audience uses it, you earn too. Want me to send you the details?
