# Ask Anyway — Influencer Targeting Strategy

Date: 2026-03-20
Status: Active — use this before outreach

---

## The Core Principle

Ignore follower count as a primary filter. What you want is a creator whose audience trusts them on hard topics. A first responder with 6,000 followers who talks about mental health on shift is worth more to this program than a wellness creator with 200,000 followers talking about self-care aesthetics.

The question to ask about any creator before you reach out:
**Does their audience already have hard conversations with them in the comments?**

If yes, they convert. If no, they don't — regardless of size.

## Priority Niches (in order)

These map directly to the audience buckets the product is built around.

### Tier 1: Start here — highest trust, highest need, most underserved

| Niche | Why It Converts | What They Post |
|---|---|---|
| First responders (police, fire, EMS) | High stigma, high need, no good resources, community loyalty | Shift life, stress, dark humor, peer support |
| Healthcare workers (nurses, EMTs, hospital staff) | Burnout is normalized, they see crisis daily, they want tools | "Day in the life," moral injury, patient loss |
| Military and veterans | Peer credibility is everything, VA resources are inadequate | Service stories, transition, mental health stigma |
| Mental health educators (therapists, counselors, social workers on TikTok) | Built-in credibility, audiences already primed, high conversion | Explaining disorders, destigmatizing therapy, client stories |

### Tier 2: Strong secondary — larger pool, slightly lower conversion

| Niche | Why It Converts | What They Post |
|---|---|---|
| Educators and teachers | They see it in students, they feel undertrained | Classroom stress, student mental health, teacher burnout |
| Parents of teens and young adults | Desperately want tools to talk to their kids | Parenting anxiety, family conflict, "how do I help my kid" |
| Single parents | High stress, limited support network, looking for practical tools | Survival mode content, loneliness, daily overwhelm |
| Addiction and recovery | Program directly addresses co-occurring patterns | Sobriety milestones, recovery identity, relapse support |

### Tier 3: Reach — broader mental health audience, lower specificity

| Niche | Notes |
|---|---|
| General mental health TikTok | Big pool, high competition, lower conversion because audience is casual |
| Christian mental health | Strong if creator frames mental health through faith lens |
| LGBTQ+ wellness | Works if creator specifically addresses mental health, not just identity |
| Neurodivergent/ADHD/Autism | Strong engagement, high sharing behavior, community oriented |

## Account Size — What to Target

### Phase 1 (launch, first 60 days): Nano and micro only

| Tier | Follower Range | Why to Start Here |
|---|---|---|
| Nano | 2,000 – 10,000 | Highest engagement rates (5-15%), easy to close, free product is enough incentive, post within a week |
| Micro | 10,000 – 75,000 | Best conversion-to-effort ratio, audience is engaged but not parasocial, creators are still accessible |

Do not go after mid-tier or macro creators at launch. They require free product + fee + approval process + exclusivity negotiations. You don't have conversion data yet to justify that spend.

### Phase 2 (after first 20 conversions): Add mid-tier

| Tier | Follower Range | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Mid | 75,000 – 300,000 | Approach only with conversion proof ("here's what nano/micro creators are earning") |

## How to Find Creators

### TikTok Search
Search these hashtags and scroll the top and recent tabs. Look for creators who show up consistently across multiple searches:
- `#mentalhealthtiktok`
- `#firstrespondertiktok` / `#firetiktok` / `#policetiktok` / `#emstiktok`
- `#nursetiktok` / `#nursesoftiktok` / `#healthcareworker`
- `#veterantiktok` / `#militarymentalhealth`
- `#teachertiktok` / `#teachermentalhealth`
- `#recoverytiktok` / `#sobrietytiktok`
- `#therapisttiktok` / `#mentalhealtheducation`

### What to Look for in Their Content
- Do they talk about hard feelings without making it aesthetic?
- Do their comments have real questions and disclosures, not just "so true" reactions?
- Have they made any video that flops on views but still has deep comment engagement? (That's your person.)
- Do they respond to comments and engage in real conversation?

### Red Flags — Pass on These
- Only posts about wellness products or affiliate links (transactional audience)
- Uses "committed suicide" language (not safe messaging compliant — training required)
- Positions mental health as something you fix with habits, routines, or products
- Has sponsored content on every other post (audience is numb to CTAs)
- Engagement is all from giveaways or trends — no real niche

## Vetting Checklist Before Outreach

Run through this before you send the first DM:

- [ ] Follower count: 2,000–75,000
- [ ] Engagement rate: 3%+ (likes + comments ÷ followers)
- [ ] Posts about mental health at least 3x/month
- [ ] Evidence of real audience trust in comments (disclosures, thank yous, "this helped me tell someone")
- [ ] No obvious safe messaging violations in recent posts
- [ ] Their audience matches at least one of the 17 product buckets
- [ ] They are a real person, not a media account or content aggregator
