# Lead Magnet: "What to Say" Pocket Guide

## Concept

A free, high-value 5–7 page PDF that solves one specific, urgent problem:
**"Someone I care about might be struggling. What do I actually say?"**

This is the most common Google search, the most common DM, the most common paralyzed feeling people have. The lead magnet answers it immediately  - and makes the buyer want the full program.

---

## Title Options

1. **"What to Say When You're Worried About Someone: A Plain-Language Guide to the Most Important Conversation"**
2. **"Say Something: Your 5-Step Guide to Starting a Suicide Conversation"**
3. **"The Script Nobody Taught You: How to Ask If Someone Is Thinking About Suicide"**
4. **"Ask Anyway: The 5-Minute Guide to the Conversation That Could Save a Life"** *(recommended  - short, action-oriented, TikTok-friendly)*

## Contents (5–7 pages)

### Page 1  - Why This Guide Exists
Brief, personal note from you (the psych NP). "I've had this conversation hundreds of times. Here's what I know works." Builds instant credibility and trust. Sets tone: clinical but human.

### Page 2  - The One Thing Most People Get Wrong
"Are you okay?" is the wrong question. It gives people an easy out. Here's why direct language works better  - and what the research says. Short, punchy, myth-busting.

### Page 3  - The Words: A Ready-to-Use Script
Exactly what to say, in three scenarios:
- Opening the conversation ("I've noticed you seem different lately, and I care about you. Can I ask you something directly?")
- The direct ask ("Are you thinking about suicide?")
- If they say yes ("Thank you for telling me. I'm not going anywhere. Can you tell me more?")

Formatted as a pull-quote card  - something people screenshot and save.

### Page 4  - What NOT to Say (Quick Reference)
The 5 most common responses that accidentally shut the conversation down. Short, scannable, honest about why each one backfires. This is the most shareable page.

### Page 5  - If They Confirm: Your Next 3 Steps
1. Stay and listen  - don't end the conversation
2. Ask the three safety questions (plan, access, intent  - simplified)
3. Connect them to help (988, how to make the call together)

### Page 6  - Crisis Resources (Always-On Reference)
- 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text)
- Crisis Text Line: text HOME to 741741
- International Association for Suicide Prevention directory
- How to find a therapist (Psychology Today, Open Path)

### Page 7  - What's Next (Soft CTA)
"This guide gives you the starting point. The full program walks you through the entire conversation  - from opening the door to safety planning together. Here's what's inside:" (brief bulleted preview of the 3-part framework + link)

## Design Notes

- Brand colors: calming but not clinical  - avoid hospital white/blue. Consider deep teal, warm cream, soft charcoal.
- Font: clean, readable sans-serif (Inter, Lato, or similar)
- Format: designed in Canva  - available as PDF download
- Must be mobile-readable (most users will open on their phone after clicking a TikTok link)
- Include 988 and Crisis Text Line prominently on every page (footer)

---

## Delivery Mechanism

1. Learner clicks link in TikTok bio
2. Lands on a simple opt-in page (Stan Store, ConvertKit landing page, or Linktree-style)
3. Enters name + email
4. PDF delivered immediately via email (ConvertKit automation)
5. Enters 5-email welcome sequence (see marketing-strategy.md)

---

## Conversion Goals

- Download-to-purchase rate target: 10–20% (for a warm, trust-based audience this is achievable)
- Email list is the long-term asset  - even non-buyers become future buyers or affiliate evangelists

---

## Safe Messaging Compliance

The lead magnet must follow all AFSP safe messaging guidelines:
- Language: "died by suicide" not "committed suicide"
- No method details
- 988 and Crisis Text Line on every page
- Framing is hope-focused and help-seeking, not crisis-focused or fear-based
- Does not make medical claims about outcomes
