## What This Helps With

When the headache won't quit and you still have to function — this isn't medical advice. It's a plan for getting through the day.

## What Is Happening

Body-based symptoms can feel scary and make decision-making harder in the moment.
A short protocol helps you move from alarm to control.
You're aiming for steady function, not instant perfection.

## What To Do Now

1. Name the symptom pattern and rate intensity from 1-10.
2. Run one grounding or pacing protocol for 90 seconds.
3. Choose one practical adjustment for the next hour.

## What To Say

- My system is spiking right now, and I'm using my reset plan.
- I need two minutes to stabilize and then I can continue.
- I'm not in danger right now; I'm in a surge and taking control steps.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Ignoring early symptoms until they become overwhelming.
- Making big decisions during peak body alarm.
- Skipping follow-up care when patterns repeat.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: lower the lights, drink a full glass of water, and take any prescribed medication. Don't power through — manage.
- Before bed tonight: eat something and set a consistent sleep time.
- Tomorrow: if it's still there, track what's different today — hydration, sleep, stress, caffeine — and start building the pattern.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Headache Functional Plan" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
