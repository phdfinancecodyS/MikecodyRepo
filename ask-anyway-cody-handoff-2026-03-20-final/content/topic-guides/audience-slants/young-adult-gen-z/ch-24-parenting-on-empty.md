## What This Helps With

You're trying to be a good parent while running on fumes. If you feel like you're failing at the thing that matters most, this guide is honest about what helps.

This version is written for young adult life, where identity, money, work, relationships, social comparison, and figuring out who you're all collide at once.

## What Is Happening

Young adult problems often get minimized as a phase, but the load is real. You may be building identity, trying to stay afloat financially, dealing with unstable work, comparing yourself online, and making adult decisions without much margin. That changes how stress, shame, and mental health hit.

Parenting on empty means you're out of fuel. Patience is gone. Presence is impossible. You're in survival mode. Your kids read your absence and they act out more because they feel the disconnection. You feel guilt about not being the parent you want to be.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask where this issue is tied to uncertainty about identity, stability, belonging, or comparison pressure rather than just personal weakness.
2. Pick one non-negotiable parenting anchor (bedtime, one meal together, morning check-in) and protect only that.
3. Release guilt about everything else. You can't do all things when you're depleted.
4. Get help with the non-anchors (school pickup, supper prep, bedtime on your off-nights).

## What To Say

- I'm not behind because I'm struggling. I'm carrying a lot while building a life.
- I need help that works in real life, not advice from someone pretending this stage is simple.
- I'm running on empty and I can only show up for [one specific thing].
- I need help with [specific tasks] so I can protect my presence for what matters most.
- When I come back to capacity, I'll be more available. Right now, this is what I can do.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Treating social comparison and instability like they're minor background issues when they're actively driving stress.
- Trying to fix the whole relationship in one conversation.
- Having the conversation when neither person is regulated enough for it.
- Skipping clear repair, boundaries, or follow-up after the hard moment.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: give yourself one 15-minute break today. Not when the kids are asleep — before that. Let something be messy.
- Before bed tonight: name one thing you did right as a parent today, even if it was small.
- Tomorrow: ask for one specific help from one specific person. Not "I need help" — "can you take them for an hour?"
- Connection support step: text, DM, or voice-note one person you actually trust today — you don't have to be fine, and you don't have to explain the whole backstory before they can show up for you.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Parenting on Empty" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
