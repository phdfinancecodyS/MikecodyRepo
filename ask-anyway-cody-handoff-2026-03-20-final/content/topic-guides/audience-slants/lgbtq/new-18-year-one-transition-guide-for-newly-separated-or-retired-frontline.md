## What This Helps With

The first year out is the hardest. Nobody prepares you for the identity loss, the boredom, and the grief of leaving. This guide walks through it month by month.

This version is written with LGBTQ+ life in mind, including identity stress, belonging, safety, chosen family, and the extra labor of figuring out where you can fully exhale.

## What Is Happening

Mental health problems don't land in a vacuum. If you're LGBTQ+, the issue may be sitting on top of identity stress, rejection history, minority stress, hiding, code-switching, or exhaustion from always reading whether a space is safe. That doesn't mean your identity is the problem. It means the context matters.

Leaving the job (through separation or retirement) is an identity loss. Your routine vanishes, your sense of purpose gets muddled, and suddenly you've space and no structure in it. The first year is disorientation, not failure. You're rebuilding how you see yourself outside the role.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this problem is being made worse by unsafety, hiding, isolation, or being around people who don't actually let you be known.
2. Don't search for a replacement identity in year one—just stabilize the basics (sleep, eat, move).
3. Keep one connection to the community (coffee with someone from work, volunteer in adjacent field, something).
4. Don't fill all the time instantly; boredom is actually part of how your nervous system recalibrates.

## What To Say

- I need support that doesn't make me shrink, explain myself, or scan the room first.
- Part of what makes this harder is the environment, not just me.
- I'm no longer in the role and I'm figuring out who I'm now.
- This isn't laziness or depression—this is transition and it takes time.
- I'm staying connected to the community in smaller ways while I settle.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Treating identity stress like it's irrelevant background noise when it's actively shaping the problem.
- Treating every problem like it must be solved tonight.
- Avoiding the issue until it gets bigger.
- Trying to carry this alone without one support check-in.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: write down three things you miss about the old life and three things you'd like to build in the new one.
- Before bed tonight: connect with one person who's been through this transition ahead of you.
- Tomorrow: do one thing that belongs to the new chapter. Sign up for something, explore something, start something small.
- Affirming support step: connect with one person or space where you don't have to justify or explain yourself before you can be supported — chosen family, a community group, or one person who already gets it.
