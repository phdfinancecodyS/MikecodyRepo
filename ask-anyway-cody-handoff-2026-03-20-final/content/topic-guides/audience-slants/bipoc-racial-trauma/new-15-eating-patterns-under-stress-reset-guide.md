## What This Helps With

You're either not eating or eating everything. Stress rewires your hunger signals. This guide helps you get back to baseline without a diet plan.

This version is written with racial stress, cultural pressure, belonging, vigilance, and the impact of systemic harm in mind.

## What Is Happening

Mental health problems can get heavier when they sit on top of racism, code-switching, cultural pressure, isolation, or the constant question of whether a space is safe. That doesn't mean everything is about race. It means race and racial stress may be increasing the load in ways that matter.

When you're under stress, eating becomes either invisible (you don't eat) or a coping tool (you eat to manage feelings). Neither is about hunger. Your job isn't to fix your eating perfectly—it's to create one pattern you can repeat when stressed that keeps your body fueled.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask where this problem gets worse because you're carrying extra vigilance, pressure to hold it together, or lack of safe understanding.
2. Pick one meal you'll face directly when stressed (breakfast, lunch, or dinner).
3. Make it simple and don't require willpower (PB&J, cereal, pasta, whatever).
4. Eat that meal at the same time every day, even if small.

## What To Say

- Part of this is personal, and part of this is the environment I'm trying to function inside.
- I need support that sees the full context, not just my symptoms.
- My eating goes sideways when I'm stressed and I'm not going to fix that by willpower alone.
- I'm committing to one meal that works when I'm overwhelmed.
- This is about keeping my body functional, not being perfect.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Treating systemic stress like it's irrelevant background instead of part of the real load you're carrying.
- Trying to quit a loop with no replacement routine.
- Using shame as motivation.
- Treating one slip like total failure.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: eat one real thing in the next hour. Not perfect, just real food.
- Before bed tonight: notice whether you over-ate or under-ate today. No judgment — just notice.
- Tomorrow: set three meal alarms. Eat when they go off whether you're hungry or not. Your hunger signals are offline — override them.
- Community support step: connect with one person or space that actually understands what you carry — where you don't have to explain the racial or cultural layer before getting to the real support.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Eating Patterns Under Stress Reset Guide" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
