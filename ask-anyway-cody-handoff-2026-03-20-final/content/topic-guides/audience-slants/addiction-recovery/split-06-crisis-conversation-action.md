## What This Helps With

Someone you care about is in crisis. You don't need to be a therapist. You need to know what to say and what to do right now. Here it is.

This version is written for people dealing with addiction, compulsive coping, sobriety, recovery, relapse fear, secrecy, and the work of building a life that doesn't depend on numbing.

## What Is Happening

Addiction and recovery change how mental health problems feel because the urge to numb, escape, control, or disappear is always close by. Even if substances or compulsions aren't the main issue today, recovery thinking, shame, secrecy, and relapse fear can still shape the problem hard.

This topic sits in the safety-first lane: when pain gets loud, your first job is connection and immediate support.
People often wait for certainty before acting. In this lane, you act early and keep someone connected.
You aren't trying to be perfect. You're trying to keep the next hour safer.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this issue is waking up urges to numb, isolate, hide, or tell yourself one slip doesn't matter.
2. Name what you aren'ticing with direct and calm language.
3. Ask one clear safety question and stay present for the answer.
4. Connect to crisis support now (988/741741/911 as needed) and don't leave them isolated.

## What To Say

- This is the kind of moment that can push me toward old coping, so I need to move early.
- I don't need to handle this alone or in secret.
- I care about you, and I want to ask something directly so we can keep you safe.
- Are you thinking about suicide right now?
- We can take one step together right now. I'm staying with you while we reach support.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Waiting until the urge is loud before using support, structure, or accountability.
- Waiting for the perfect wording while risk rises.
- Debating whether pain is serious enough before offering support.
- Putting paid resources ahead of immediate safety actions.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: identify the person you're worried about and decide: am I going to ask today or not?
- Before bed tonight: practice your opening sentence out loud. "I care about you. Can I ask you something real?"
- Tomorrow: ask. Not perfectly. Not with a script. Just ask.
- Recovery support step: reach out to your sponsor, home group, or one person from your program today — not to explain everything, just to make contact.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Crisis Conversation Action" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
