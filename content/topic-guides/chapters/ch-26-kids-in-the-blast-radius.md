## What This Helps With

Your kids are absorbing more than you think. This guide helps you see what they're picking up and what to do about it, without the guilt spiral.

## What Is Happening

Kids in the blast radius means your kids are reading your dysregulation, your conflict, your exhaustion. They may not understand it consciously, but they feel it. Their behavior, sleep, and emotional regulation are affected by your nervous system's output.

## What To Do Now

1. Name for your kids what's happening: I'm struggling right now and this isn't your fault.
2. Model one reset when they see you dysregulated (I'm frustrated so I'm taking a walk).
3. Give them one thing they can do when they see you struggling (hug you, leave you alone, get you water).

## What To Say: Exact Scripts and Phrases

Here are the exact words and phrases to use:


- My struggle is real and you aren't the cause.
- When I do [reset action], it helps me come back to okay.
- You're safe. My feelings are big, and I'm taking care of them.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Pretending everything is fine when your kids can already see that it isn't.
- Overexplaining adult problems to kids who just need to know they're safe.
- Spiraling into guilt about what they've already seen instead of starting repair now.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: watch your kids for five minutes without fixing anything. Just notice what they're doing and how they seem.
- Before bed tonight: think about what your kids have been exposed to in the last month and write down what they might be carrying.
- Tomorrow: ask one child one real question and listen without redirecting. "How are you doing with all of this?"

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: See what your kids are absorbing and where the impact is showing.

Prompts:
- What have your kids been exposed to in the last month, yelling, tension, withdrawal, unpredictability?
- How is each child reacting, acting out, going quiet, clinging, pretending everything's fine?
- What do your kids see when you're dysregulated? Be honest about what they witness.
- Have you talked to them about what's happening, or are you hoping they haven't noticed?
- What's one thing your kid has said or done recently that tells you they're carrying something?
