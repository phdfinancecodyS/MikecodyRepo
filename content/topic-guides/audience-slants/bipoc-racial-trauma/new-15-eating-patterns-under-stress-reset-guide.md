## What This Helps With

You're either not eating or eating everything. Stress rewires your hunger signals. This guide helps you get back to baseline without a diet plan.
This version is written with racial stress, cultural pressure, belonging, vigilance, and the impact of systemic harm in mind.

## What Is Happening

Mental health problems can get heavier when they sit on top of racism, code-switching, cultural pressure, isolation, or the constant question of whether a space is safe. That doesn't mean everything is about race. It means race and racial stress may be increasing the load in ways that matter.

When you're under stress, eating becomes either invisible (you don't eat) or a coping tool (you eat to manage feelings). Neither is about hunger. Your job isn't to fix your eating perfectly: it's to create one pattern you can repeat when stressed that keeps your body fueled.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask where this problem gets worse because you're carrying extra vigilance, pressure to hold it together, or lack of safe understanding.
2. Pick one meal you'll face directly when stressed (breakfast, lunch, or dinner).
3. Make it simple and don't require willpower (PB&J, cereal, pasta, whatever).
4. Eat that meal at the same time every day, even if small.

## What To Say

- Part of this is personal, and part of this is the environment I'm trying to function inside.
- I need support that sees the full context, not just my symptoms.
- My eating goes sideways when I'm stressed and I'm not going to fix that by willpower alone.
- I'm committing to one meal that works when I'm overwhelmed.
- This is about keeping my body functional, not being perfect.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Treating systemic stress like it's irrelevant background instead of part of the real load you're carrying.
- Starting a restrictive diet during a crisis. Your body needs fuel right now, not a new rule set to fail at.
- Using shame about eating as motivation. Shame makes the pattern worse, not better.
- Treating skipped meals as discipline. If you're not eating, you're not functioning: your brain needs glucose to regulate mood.
- Bingeing and then restricting to "make up for it." That cycle is the problem, not the solution.
- Expecting your eating to normalize before your stress does. Eating follows stress: manage the stress and the eating starts to settle.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: eat one real thing in the next hour. Not perfect, just real food.
- Before bed tonight: notice whether you over-ate or under-ate today. No judgment: just notice.
- Tomorrow: set three meal alarms. Eat when they go off whether you're hungry or not. Your hunger signals are offline: override them.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits bipoc / racial trauma life.

## Worksheet 1: Stress-Eating Pattern Map

Goal: Understand exactly how stress is rewiring your eating so you can interrupt the pattern at the right point.

Prompts:
- When you're stressed, which direction does your eating go? (not eating, overeating, eating junk only, eating at random times, forgetting to eat entirely?)
- What does your eating look like on a typical high-stress day? Walk through breakfast/lunch/dinner. What did you actually eat yesterday?
- What feelings or situations trigger the pattern? (overwhelm, boredom, anger, numbness, shift work, not having time?)
- How is your body responding to the current eating pattern? (energy crashes, weight changes, stomach issues, mood swings, can't sleep?)
- What's your relationship with food like right now? One sentence. Be honest.
- Where do you feel most pressure to stay composed, guarded, or unreadable?

## Worksheet 2: One-Meal Reset Plan

Goal: Commit to one repeatable meal that serves as your anchor when stress takes over.

Prompts:
- The one meal I'll commit to eating every day no matter what: ___ (breakfast, lunch, or dinner). The specific food: ___ (keep it dead simple: PB&J, cereal, eggs, whatever requires zero decision-making).
- I'll eat this meal at: ___ every day. I'm setting an alarm for it: yes/no.
- The thing I'll stop doing this week: ___ (skipping meals entirely, eating in the car, eating only snacks, eating at midnight).
- When I notice the stress-eating pattern starting, I'll do this first: ___ (drink water, pause for 60 seconds, ask "am I hungry or am I stressed?").
- By the end of this week, I'll know the reset is working if: ___ (more energy, fewer crashes, eating at least two real meals a day, stomach calming down).
