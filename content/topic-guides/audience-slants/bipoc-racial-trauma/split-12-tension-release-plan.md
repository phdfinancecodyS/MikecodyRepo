## What This Helps With

Your body is holding everything your brain won't say. Jaw, shoulders, chest, gut. Here's how to let some of it go without needing a reason.
This version is written with racial stress, cultural pressure, belonging, vigilance, and the impact of systemic harm in mind.

## What Is Happening

Mental health problems can get heavier when they sit on top of racism, code-switching, cultural pressure, isolation, or the constant question of whether a space is safe. That doesn't mean everything is about race. It means race and racial stress may be increasing the load in ways that matter.

Your body is keeping score. Every unspoken word, every swallowed reaction, every moment you held it together: your muscles stored it. Jaw clenching, shoulder tension, chest tightness, stomach knots, fists you don't remember making. This is your autonomic nervous system holding a stress position 24/7.

The tension isn't random. It's communication. Your body is telling you what your brain won't say.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask where this problem gets worse because you're carrying extra vigilance, pressure to hold it together, or lack of safe understanding.
2. Scan your body right now: jaw, shoulders, chest, fists, gut. Where are you holding?
3. Deliberately tense the area for 5 seconds, then release completely. Feel the difference.
4. Set three check-in alarms throughout the day: scan, release, breathe.

## What To Say

- Part of this is personal, and part of this is the environment I'm trying to function inside.
- I need support that sees the full context, not just my symptoms.
- I've been clenching all day and I didn't even notice until now.
- My body is holding something I haven't dealt with yet.
- I need to let some of this go physically before I can deal with it any other way.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Treating systemic stress like it's irrelevant background instead of part of the real load you're carrying.
- Ignoring chronic tension until it becomes pain, headaches, or TMJ.
- Trying to relax your body while your life stays at full speed: the tension is a symptom.
- Only doing relaxation when it's already bad instead of building daily release into your routine.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: scan from your jaw to your feet. Where are you holding tension? Drop your shoulders. Unclench your jaw. Relax your fists.
- Before bed tonight: do 60 seconds of progressive muscle relaxation: tense and release each group.
- Tomorrow: set three check-in alarms throughout the day. Each one: scan, release, breathe.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits bipoc / racial trauma life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Map where your body holds stress and what it's connected to.

Prompts:
- Where does your body hold tension most: jaw, shoulders, chest, stomach, hands, back?
- When is the tension worst: morning, after work, during conflict, at night?
- What emotion are you not expressing that your body might be storing?
- How long has the chronic tension been there? Has it gotten worse recently?
- What happens when you try to relax: can you, or does your body resist?
- Where do you feel most pressure to stay composed, guarded, or unreadable?
