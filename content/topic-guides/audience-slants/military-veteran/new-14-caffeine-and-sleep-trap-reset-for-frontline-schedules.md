## What This Helps With

Coffee to function, can't sleep because of the coffee, more coffee tomorrow. If that's your life, here's how to break the cycle without quitting cold turkey.
This version is written for military and veteran life, where mission, hypervigilance, identity, loss of tribe, and carrying the past all shape how a problem lands.

## What Is Happening

Military life trains your body and mind around vigilance, control, performance, and mission. Even after service, those patterns don't disappear just because the environment changed. A lot of the struggle isn't weakness. It's training, injury, loss, and identity still showing up in civilian life.

Shift work + caffeine + sleep debt creates a trap: you need caffeine to function in the shift, caffeine keeps you wired when you finally sleep, poor sleep makes you need more caffeine. Breaking this cycle means picking one variable to shift and holding it for two weeks.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this reaction belongs to your current environment or to training and survival patterns that are still firing after the mission changed.
2. Choose: cut caffeine after noon, or shift your caffeine timing two hours earlier, or none (hardest).
3. Commit to that one change for 14 days no matter what.
4. On days off, don't use caffeine as a catch-up tool: your body is trying to recalibrate.

## What To Say

- Part of this is service-related, and I'm not going to shame myself for the fact that it still shows up.
- I need support that respects the way training and loss changed me.
- I'm caught in caffeine and sleep patterns that aren't working.
- For the next two weeks I'm trying [one specific change]. That's the only thing I'm changing.
- This will be rough and then it will settle.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Expecting civilian routines, relationships, or workplaces to make sense without accounting for service, transition, or loss of tribe.
- Using caffeine to override fatigue instead of addressing the sleep debt underneath it. The fix isn't more stimulant: it's better sleep architecture.
- Quitting caffeine cold turkey on a day off and spending the whole day with a withdrawal headache and brain fog. Taper: don't crash.
- Changing your entire routine at once: new diet, no caffeine, new sleep schedule, new workout. One variable at a time for two weeks.
- Drinking energy drinks because they "don't count" as coffee. They count. 200mg is 200mg regardless of the can.
- Ignoring that your shift schedule is the actual structural problem and expecting a caffeine fix to compensate for a system that makes sleep impossible.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: note what time it is. If it's after 2pm, switch to water. Start there.
- Before bed tonight: write down how many caffeine servings you had today and when the last one was.
- Tomorrow: push your last caffeine 30 minutes earlier than today. Not cold turkey: just 30 minutes.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits military / veteran life.

## Worksheet 1: Caffeine-Sleep Cycle Tracker

Goal: Map your actual caffeine and sleep pattern so you can see the trap clearly.

Prompts:
- How many caffeine servings do you have in a typical 24-hour period? (count everything: coffee, energy drinks, pre-workout, soda, tea)
- What time is your last caffeine? What time do you try to sleep? What's the gap?
- Rate your sleep quality 1–10 for the last week. On your worst nights, what time did you finally fall asleep?
- What does your body feel like at 3 PM? What about at midnight? Describe the energy cycle specifically.
- What have you tried before to fix the sleep? What happened? (Melatonin, cutting caffeine, sleep meds, quitting entirely?)
- Where does training still help you, and where is it creating friction in civilian life or close relationships?

## Worksheet 2: Two-Week Caffeine Reset Plan

Goal: Pick one change, commit for 14 days, and track the results.

Prompts:
- The one change I'm making for 14 days: ___ (no caffeine after noon / push last caffeine 30 min earlier each day / switch to half-caf after 10 AM).
- My current last-caffeine time is: ___. By day 14, my target last-caffeine time is: ___.
- On days off, I'll do this instead of extra caffeine: ___ (nap before 2 PM, walk, cold water, nothing: just be tired).
- I'll track my sleep quality each night on a 1–10 scale in: ___ (notes app, journal, calendar).
- The hardest part will be: ___. My plan for that moment: ___ (drink water, power through the slump, go for a 5-minute walk).
