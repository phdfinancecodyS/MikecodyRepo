## What This Helps With

When your world shrinks to one thing and you can't see anything else: that's tunnel vision. Here's how to widen the lens before you make a decision you'll regret.
This version is written for people dealing with addiction, compulsive coping, sobriety, recovery, relapse fear, secrecy, and the work of building a life that doesn't depend on numbing.

## What Is Happening

Addiction and recovery change how mental health problems feel because the urge to numb, escape, control, or disappear is always close by. Even if substances or compulsions aren't the main issue today, recovery thinking, shame, secrecy, and relapse fear can still shape the problem hard.

Tunnel vision is your brain's threat-response narrowing your world to one thing: one problem, one fear, one outcome: and blocking everything else out. It's the stress equivalent of looking through a paper towel roll: you see one thing in sharp focus and lose the entire picture.

This happens when your nervous system decides something is urgent. Your prefrontal cortex (the planning, reasoning part) goes offline and your amygdala takes the wheel. The result: bad decisions, all-or-nothing thinking, and a conviction that there's only one way out.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this issue is waking up urges to numb, isolate, hide, or tell yourself one slip doesn't matter.
2. Name the one thing your brain is locked on: "I'm tunneled on ______."
3. Force peripheral input: list three other things that are also true right now.
4. Delay any major decision by 24 hours. Tunnel vision is a terrible advisor.

## What To Say

- This is the kind of moment that can push me toward old coping, so I need to move early.
- I don't need to handle this alone or in secret.
- I can't see past this one thing right now and I know that's the stress talking.
- I need to widen the lens before I decide anything.
- Can you tell me what else is going on that I'm not seeing?

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Waiting until the urge is loud before using support, structure, or accountability.
- Making irreversible decisions while your brain is locked on one outcome.
- Convincing yourself the tunnel vision IS clarity: it's not, it's panic with focus.
- Refusing input from others because "you already know" what to do.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: name the one thing your brain is stuck on. Write it down.
- Before bed tonight: list three other things that are also true right now: things your brain is ignoring.
- Tomorrow: make one decision that accounts for all four things, not just the one your brain is screaming about.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits addiction / recovery life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Catch the narrowing: what your brain locks onto and what it's blocking out.

Prompts:
- What's the one thing your brain is stuck on right now? Write it down.
- What are three other things that are also true right now that your brain is ignoring?
- When did the tunnel start: and what was the trigger?
- What decision are you about to make from inside the tunnel? Would you make the same one tomorrow?
- Who are you not listening to right now because you're sure you already know?
- What thoughts, feelings, or situations around this problem make you want to numb out, hide, or go back to old coping?
