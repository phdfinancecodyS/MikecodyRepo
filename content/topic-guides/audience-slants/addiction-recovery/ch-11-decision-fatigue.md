## What This Helps With

When even small choices feel impossible and your brain just wants someone else to decide: that's decision fatigue. Here's how to get through the day without burning out on breakfast.
This version is written for people dealing with addiction, compulsive coping, sobriety, recovery, relapse fear, secrecy, and the work of building a life that doesn't depend on numbing.

## What Is Happening

Addiction and recovery change how mental health problems feel because the urge to numb, escape, control, or disappear is always close by. Even if substances or compulsions aren't the main issue today, recovery thinking, shame, secrecy, and relapse fear can still shape the problem hard.

Decision fatigue hits after you've made too many choices and your brain's decision-making fuel is empty. That last small decision (what to eat, which shirt) feels impossible. Your nervous system shuts down the decision-making system to protect itself. You feel stuck and helpless because your brain literally can't decide.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this issue is waking up urges to numb, isolate, hide, or tell yourself one slip doesn't matter.
2. Cut decisions. Have a default for recurring choices (same breakfast, uniform clothes).
3. Make important decisions early in the day when your fuel is full.
4. For the rest of the day, use a coin flip or ask someone else to choose for non-critical decisions.

## What To Say

- This is the kind of moment that can push me toward old coping, so I need to move early.
- I don't need to handle this alone or in secret.
- I've hit decision fatigue and I can't choose right now.
- Can you decide this one? I'm out of mental fuel.
- I'm moving this decision to tomorrow when my brain is fresher.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Waiting until the urge is loud before using support, structure, or accountability.
- Making important decisions in the afternoon when your decision fuel is already gone.
- Trying to optimize every choice instead of making a fast-enough choice and moving on.
- Adding more options to a decision when your brain needs fewer, not more.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: make one decision that's been sitting and do it in the next 10 minutes. It doesn't matter if it's perfect.
- Before bed tonight: pre-decide three things for tomorrow (what to eat, what to wear, what to start with).
- Tomorrow: limit yourself to two real decisions before noon. Everything else is autopilot.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits addiction / recovery life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern: when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- What type of decisions drain you most: big ones, small ones, or all of them?
- What do you do when you can't decide? Freeze, avoid, or let someone else choose?
- How many decisions do you estimate you make before noon?
- What's one decision you've been avoiding that's taking up mental space?
- When decision-making was easier, what was different about your life?
- What thoughts, feelings, or situations around this problem make you want to numb out, hide, or go back to old coping?
