## What This Helps With

When the thing that defined you ends: the job, the uniform, the role: and you don't know who you are without it. This is about that transition.
This version stays broad and practical so it works if you don't want a specific identity lens on the problem.

## What Is Happening

A lot of mental health problems get worse when people overcomplicate them or wait too long to act. The goal is to name the pattern clearly, understand it in plain language, and take one useful next step.

Identity after role is the crisis of losing the role that organized your sense of self. You were a soldier, a cop, a firefighter, a healthcare worker. Now you aren't. Your title is gone. Your mission is gone. Your sense of who you're got tangled in that role. Without it, you don't know who you are.

## What To Do Now

1. Strip the problem down to what's actually happening this week, not the whole story of your life.
2. Separate your identity from the role: You had values and skills before the role and they don't disappear with the title.
3. Write down the parts of yourself that exist outside the role (as a friend, parent, partner, citizen).
4. Find new structure that provides mission and meaning (not the same role, but something that matters).

## What To Say

- This is real, and I can work with what's in front of me right now.
- I don't need a perfect plan to take the next useful step.
- I've lost the role that organized my identity and I'm rebuilding who I am.
- My values and skills don't disappear when I step out of uniform.
- I'm finding new ways to contribute and new identity anchors.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Staying vague about the problem so long that you can't act on it.
- Trying to replace the old role with an identical new one instead of rebuilding identity from values.
- Introducing yourself by what you used to be instead of who you're becoming.
- Numbing the identity loss with busyness instead of grieving the transition honestly.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: finish this sentence: "Before, I was ______. Now I'm ______." Write it down.
- Before bed tonight: list three things about yourself that have nothing to do with the old role.
- Tomorrow: do one thing that belongs to the new version of you. Not the old role. Not someone else's expectations. Yours.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits general mental health life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Map the gap between who you were inside the role and who you're becoming without it.

Prompts:
- What role defined you (soldier, officer, provider, caregiver, leader)? What did it give you?
- What parts of your identity survived when the role ended? What parts didn't?
- When someone asks "What do you do?": what happens inside you?
- What do you miss most: the purpose, the structure, the status, or the belonging?
- What would it mean to be valuable without a title attached to your name?
- What part of this problem is most active in your life right now?
