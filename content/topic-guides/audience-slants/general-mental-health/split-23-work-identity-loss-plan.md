## What This Helps With

If your sense of self was built around a job you no longer have: and now you don't know who you are: this guide is for that disorientation.
This version stays broad and practical so it works if you don't want a specific identity lens on the problem.

## What Is Happening

A lot of mental health problems get worse when people overcomplicate them or wait too long to act. The goal is to name the pattern clearly, understand it in plain language, and take one useful next step.

Work and identity stress often blends pressure, uncertainty, and loss of control.
Clarity improves when you separate what's urgent, what's important, and what can wait.
Small structure beats constant rumination.

## What To Do Now

1. Strip the problem down to what's actually happening this week, not the whole story of your life.
2. Write the top three stress points in plain words.
3. Pick one controllable action for today.
4. Set one boundary that protects energy this week.

## What To Say

- This is real, and I can work with what's in front of me right now.
- I don't need a perfect plan to take the next useful step.
- I'm under strain and I want to handle this with a clear plan.
- Here's the one piece I can control today.
- I'm setting this boundary so I can stay functional and reliable.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Staying vague about the problem so long that you can't act on it.
- Panicking and grabbing the first job offer that shows up because the gap feels unbearable.
- Defining yourself entirely by the job you lost. You had a role: you are not the role.
- Isolating from people because you're embarrassed about what happened. Silence breeds shame; connection breaks it.
- Spending weeks "job searching" without actually applying because the rejection fear is running the show.
- Ignoring the grief of the loss. Losing a work identity is a real loss: even if you hated the job.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: write down three things you're good at that have nothing to do with your old job.
- Before bed tonight: ask yourself what you miss most: the work, the people, or the identity? They're different.
- Tomorrow: do one thing that builds toward whatever comes next. Not the perfect thing. Just a step.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits general mental health life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Map the loss: what the job gave you and what's missing now.

Prompts:
- What did the job give you besides money: purpose, identity, structure, status, belonging?
- When someone asks "what do you do?" what happens inside you?
- What have you been doing to fill the time? Is it working or just filling?
- What skill or value of yours has nothing to do with a job title?
- What scares you most about what comes next: failure, irrelevance, starting over, or being seen?
- What part of this problem is most active in your life right now?

## Worksheet 2: Action Builder

Goal: Take one step toward what's next: not a career plan, just one step.

Prompts:
- One thing I'll do this week that uses my skills outside of a job (help someone, build something, volunteer):
- The sentence I'll practice: "I used to be ______. I'm figuring out what's next."
- One person who sees me as more than my job title that I'll talk to:
- One small structure I'll add to my days (wake time, routine, project, appointment):
- By next week, I'll know this worked if:
