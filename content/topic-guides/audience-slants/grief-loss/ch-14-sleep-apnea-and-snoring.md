## What This Helps With

If you're waking up exhausted, snoring through the night, or your partner says you stop breathing: this isn't just annoying. Here's what to do about it.
This version is written for people carrying grief, fresh loss, old loss that is still active, or the kind of missing that changes how every other problem lands.

## What Is Happening

Grief changes the shape of everything. It lowers capacity, intensifies loneliness, scrambles concentration, and can make normal stress feel enormous. It also doesn't move in a straight line. The goal isn't to rush you past grief. It's to help you function while grief is still part of the room.

Sleep apnea disrupts sleep architecture so you never reach deep sleep. You wake dozens of times per night without knowing it. You feel like you slept but you're still exhausted. This exhaustion affects everything: mood, attention, reactivity. It isn't something you can willpower through.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask how much of this problem is being amplified by grief load, anniversaries, reminders, or the effort of trying to function while carrying absence.
2. If you snore, have gaps in breathing, or wake gasping: get a sleep study done.
3. If diagnosed with apnea, get the CPAP machine and commit to using it. Your brain won't recover without it.
4. Give the machine 3 weeks before judging it. Your body needs time to adjust.

## What To Say

- This problem isn't happening separate from grief. Grief is part of the load right now.
- I don't need to be over this to take a useful next step.
- My sleep disruption is medical, not behavioral.
- I got diagnosed with sleep apnea and I'm treating it.
- Recovery happens gradually but my waking life will improve significantly once my sleep is fixed.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Judging yourself for reduced capacity without accounting for the real weight of loss.
- Ignoring physical warning signs until they take over the whole day.
- Pushing through symptoms as if force will solve a nervous-system problem.
- Treating body symptoms like a character problem instead of a signal.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: if you've been putting off a sleep study, call and schedule it today. Just the call.
- Before bed tonight: sleep on your side instead of your back. Use a pillow barrier if you need to.
- Tomorrow: track how many times you woke up gasping or how tired you feel at noon. Start building the data.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits grief / loss life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Track the signs: what your body is telling you and what you've been ignoring.

Prompts:
- How tired are you during the day on a 1–10 scale? How long has it been that way?
- Does your partner say you snore, gasp, or stop breathing at night?
- What does "tired" look like for you: brain fog, irritability, falling asleep at your desk, something else?
- Have you had a sleep study? If not, what's stopped you?
- What other symptoms might be linked: headaches, weight changes, high blood pressure?
- How is grief changing your energy, concentration, relationships, or stress tolerance right now?
