## What This Helps With

When you're asking 'where was God in that?' or your faith doesn't hold what it used to: this guide sits with those questions instead of rushing past them.
This version is written for people carrying grief, fresh loss, old loss that is still active, or the kind of missing that changes how every other problem lands.

## What Is Happening

Grief changes the shape of everything. It lowers capacity, intensifies loneliness, scrambles concentration, and can make normal stress feel enormous. It also doesn't move in a straight line. The goal isn't to rush you past grief. It's to help you function while grief is still part of the room.

Faith, God, and big questions become huge when you've seen things that violate your belief system. Why does God allow suffering? How do I reconcile faith with what I've seen? Is there meaning? Is there purpose? These aren't abstract. They're about whether your life means anything.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask how much of this problem is being amplified by grief load, anniversaries, reminders, or the effort of trying to function while carrying absence.
2. Sit with the questions without needing to answer them right now.
3. Separate faith from theology: You can have faith questions without needing doctrine answers.
4. Find community with others asking the same questions (not people with final answers, but people in the question with you).

## What To Say

- This problem isn't happening separate from grief. Grief is part of the load right now.
- I don't need to be over this to take a useful next step.
- My faith is being shaken and I'm sitting in the questions.
- I don't have answers about meaning and purpose right now and that's okay.
- I'm finding community with others who are asking these questions too.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Judging yourself for reduced capacity without accounting for the real weight of loss.
- Rushing to resolve faith questions with clean answers instead of sitting honestly in the mess.
- Letting someone else's certainty shame you out of your real doubts.
- Abandoning faith entirely because it can't explain one terrible thing: faith and questions can coexist.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: say the question you've been afraid to ask out loud. Even if it's angry. Especially if it's angry.
- Before bed tonight: sit with the question for five minutes without trying to answer it.
- Tomorrow: find one person: a friend, a spiritual leader, a counselor: and tell them where your faith is right now.
- Audience-lens support step: use one support, person, community, or routine that fits grief / loss life.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Map what changed between you and faith: the before, the break, and where you are now.

Prompts:
- What did your faith used to give you: comfort, community, identity, answers, structure?
- What broke it: a specific event, a slow erosion, or something someone said/did in God's name?
- What's the hardest part: losing the belief, losing the community, or losing who you were inside it?
- When you try to pray, attend, or practice now, what happens inside you?
- What question are you carrying that nobody in your faith tradition could answer?
- How is grief changing your energy, concentration, relationships, or stress tolerance right now?
