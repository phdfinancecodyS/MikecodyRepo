# When You Feel Like the Villain: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** Addiction / Recovery

**From guide:** When You Feel Like the Villain

---

Goal: Separate what you did from who you are: track the villain narrative.

Prompts:
- Write the story your brain tells about why you're the bad guy. All of it. Don't edit it.
- Who told you that story first: was it you, or did someone else plant it?
- What evidence does your brain collect to prove you're the villain? What evidence does it ignore?
- When the villain feeling is loudest, what do you do: withdraw, overfunction, self-punish, lash out?
- If someone you loved told you they did what you did, would you call them a villain? What would you say instead?
- What thoughts, feelings, or situations around this problem make you want to numb out, hide, or go back to old coping?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
