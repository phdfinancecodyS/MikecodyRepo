# Survivor Guilt After Calls Scenes Incidents: Survivor Guilt Inventory

**Audience:** Addiction / Recovery

**From guide:** Survivor Guilt After Calls Scenes Incidents

---

Goal: Name the specific guilt you're carrying so it stops running silently in the background.

Prompts:
- Which incident does the guilt attach to most? Describe it briefly: what happened, what your role was.
- What's the exact thought that loops? ("I should have..." "If only I had..." "Why them and not me?")
- When does the guilt get loudest? (anniversaries, similar calls, quiet moments, seeing the family?)
- What have you done to try to manage it? What worked? What made it worse?
- Has the guilt changed how you do the job? How you treat yourself? How you connect with people?
- What thoughts, feelings, or situations around this problem make you want to numb out, hide, or go back to old coping?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
