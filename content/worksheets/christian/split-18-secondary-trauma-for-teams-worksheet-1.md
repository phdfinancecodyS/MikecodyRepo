# Secondary Trauma for Teams: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** Christian

**From guide:** Secondary Trauma for Teams

---

Goal: Assess the team's weight: what you're all carrying and who's struggling.

Prompts:
- What's the heaviest thing the team has absorbed recently? Has anyone named it?
- Who on the team has gone quieter, more cynical, or more detached lately?
- What does the team do with hard calls/cases/shifts: debrief, joke, drink, or nothing?
- What's the team's unspoken rule about showing struggle? ("We don't talk about it," "Suck it up," "Only the new people struggle")
- If someone on the team was in crisis, would you know? Would they tell anyone?
- How does faith language help this problem, and where does it make you hide instead of tell the truth?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
