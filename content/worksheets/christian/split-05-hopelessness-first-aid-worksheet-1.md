# Hopelessness First Aid: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** Christian

**From guide:** Hopelessness First Aid

---

Goal: Spot the pattern: when hopelessness shows up, what feeds it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did the hopelessness hit hardest this week? What was happening right before?
- What does hopelessness feel like in your body: heavy, empty, numb, or something else?
- What thought keeps looping? Write it down word for word.
- What time of day is worst? What makes it spike: isolation, fatigue, comparison, or something specific?
- When's the last time you felt even a little bit of something that wasn't hopelessness? What were you doing?
- How does faith language help this problem, and where does it make you hide instead of tell the truth?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
