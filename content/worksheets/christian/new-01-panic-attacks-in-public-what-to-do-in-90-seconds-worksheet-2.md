# Panic Attacks in Public: What To Do in 90 Seconds: 90-Second Panic Plan Builder

**Audience:** Christian

**From guide:** Panic Attacks in Public: What To Do in 90 Seconds

---

Goal: Build a concrete plan you can execute on autopilot when the next public episode starts.

Prompts:
- My first panic signal is usually: ___. When I notice that signal, I'll immediately do: ___ (feet flat, cold object, slow exhale 4-7-8).
- The one sentence I'll say to myself mid-episode: "This is adrenaline. It peaks in 90 seconds. I've survived every one."
- One person who knows about my panic and I can text a code word to: ___. The code word is: ___.
- One public place I've been avoiding that I'll re-visit this week with an exit plan: ___. My exit plan is: ___.
- After the episode passes, I'll recover by doing this instead of going straight home: ___ (walk one block, get water, sit on a bench for five minutes).

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
