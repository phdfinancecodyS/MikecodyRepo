# Shift-Work Relationship Survival Plan: Shift-Work Relationship Pressure Points

**Audience:** Social Workers / Counselors

**From guide:** Shift-Work Relationship Survival Plan

---

Goal: Identify the specific friction points between your schedule and your relationship so you can fix the system, not the person.

Prompts:
- What's the biggest source of tension in your relationship right now that's connected to your schedule? Be specific.
- When you come home from a hard shift, what does your partner see? (silence, irritability, checking out, needing to be alone?) What do you wish they understood about that?
- What does your partner need from you that you consistently can't give because of the schedule? Name it honestly.
- When was the last time you had a real conversation: not logistics, not kids, not complaints: with your person? What made it possible?
- What's one thing your partner has said about the schedule that stuck with you, even if you didn't respond to it?
- How does your role as a helper make this pattern easier to hide or explain away?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
