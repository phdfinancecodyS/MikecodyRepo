# Year-One Transition Guide for Newly Separated or Retired Frontline: Year-One Transition Inventory

**Audience:** Military / Veteran

**From guide:** Year-One Transition Guide for Newly Separated or Retired Frontline

---

Goal: Take stock of where you actually are in the transition: not where you think you should be.

Prompts:
- How long have you been out of the role? How are you spending your days right now?
- What do you miss most about the job? What do you miss least? Be specific.
- How's your body doing? (sleep, appetite, energy, aches, restlessness?) Rate each 1–10.
- What's the hardest part of the transition that nobody warned you about?
- Who are you now that you're not [the job title]? If you can't answer that yet, write: "I don't know yet and that's where I am."
- Where does training still help you, and where is it creating friction in civilian life or close relationships?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
