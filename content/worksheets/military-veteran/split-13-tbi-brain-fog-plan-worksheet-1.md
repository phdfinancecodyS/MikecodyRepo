# TBI Brain Fog Plan: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** Military / Veteran

**From guide:** TBI Brain Fog Plan

---

Goal: Map the fog: when it's worst, what makes it better, and what you've stopped doing because of it.

Prompts:
- What does brain fog feel like for you: slow processing, lost words, blank spaces, confusion?
- What time of day is worst? What makes the fog thicker (noise, multitasking, screens, fatigue)?
- What have you given up because your brain doesn't work the way it used to?
- What do people around you notice: and do they get it or dismiss it?
- What helps: even a little: when the fog lifts? (Rest, quiet, routine, breaks?)
- Where does training still help you, and where is it creating friction in civilian life or close relationships?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
