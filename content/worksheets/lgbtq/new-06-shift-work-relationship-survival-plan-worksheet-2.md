# Shift-Work Relationship Survival Plan: Shift-Work Relationship Repair Plan

**Audience:** LGBTQ+

**From guide:** Shift-Work Relationship Survival Plan

---

Goal: Build one practical change per week that keeps your relationship functional despite the schedule.

Prompts:
- The one thing I'll do after every hard shift before engaging with my partner: ___ (shower, sit in the car for 10 minutes, walk around the block).
- One non-negotiable weekly connection time I'll protect: ___. (Even 30 minutes counts. Put it on the calendar.)
- The sentence I'll use when I need space without making my partner feel rejected: "I need ___ minutes to come down. I'm not mad at you."
- One logistics problem I'll solve this week so it stops being a fight: ___ (meal planning, kid pickup, sleeping schedules).
- The one honest thing I need to say to my partner about how the schedule affects me that I haven't said yet: ___.

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
