# Tension Release Plan: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** General Mental Health

**From guide:** Tension Release Plan

---

Goal: Map where your body holds stress and what it's connected to.

Prompts:
- Where does your body hold tension most: jaw, shoulders, chest, stomach, hands, back?
- When is the tension worst: morning, after work, during conflict, at night?
- What emotion are you not expressing that your body might be storing?
- How long has the chronic tension been there? Has it gotten worse recently?
- What happens when you try to relax: can you, or does your body resist?
- What part of this problem is most active in your life right now?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
