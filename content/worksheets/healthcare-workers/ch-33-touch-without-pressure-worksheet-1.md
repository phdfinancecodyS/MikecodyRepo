# Touch Without Pressure: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** Healthcare Workers

**From guide:** Touch Without Pressure

---

Goal: Map your relationship with touch: what's safe, what's not, and what your body needs.

Prompts:
- What kind of touch feels okay right now? What kind makes you want to pull away?
- When touch happens, does your body relax, brace, freeze, or check out?
- Does touch feel like connection or like pressure toward something else?
- When's the last time touch felt genuinely safe? What made it different?
- What does your partner think your relationship with touch is? What's the truth?
- What parts of clinical work or patient care are most connected to this pattern lately?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
