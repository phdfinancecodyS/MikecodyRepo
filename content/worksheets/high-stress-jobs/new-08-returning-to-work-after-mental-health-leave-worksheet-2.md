# Returning to Work After Mental Health Leave: First-Week-Back Survival Plan

**Audience:** High Stress Jobs

**From guide:** Returning to Work After Mental Health Leave

---

Goal: Build a concrete plan for your first week so you're not making it up on the fly while your nervous system is screaming.

Prompts:
- Day 1: My plan is to arrive at ___, leave at ___, and talk to ___ people max. My exit plan if I hit a wall: ___.
- The sentence I'll use when someone asks where I've been: "___." (Practice saying it out loud three times right now.)
- My regulation tools I'll keep accessible at work: ___ (headphones, walk break, breathing app, cold water, texting my person).
- My check-in person for this week is: ___. I'll text them at ___ each day with a one-word status.
- If I realize I came back too soon, my plan is: ___ (talk to HR, call my therapist, take a half day). Coming back too soon isn't failure: it's information.

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
