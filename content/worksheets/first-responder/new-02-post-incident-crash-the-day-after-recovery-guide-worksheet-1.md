# Post-Incident Crash: The Day-After Recovery Guide: Post-Incident Crash Tracker

**Audience:** First Responder

**From guide:** Post-Incident Crash: The Day-After Recovery Guide

---

Goal: Map what happens to your body and mind in the 24–72 hours after high-impact incidents so you can recognize the crash pattern.

Prompts:
- Describe the last incident that hit you hard. What happened? What role did you play?
- What did the crash look like the day after? (flat affect, couldn't sleep, couldn't eat, rage, crying, numb, wired?)
- How long did the crash last before you felt somewhat functional again? Days? A week? Longer?
- What did you do during the crash? What helped and what made it worse?
- Who checked on you afterward? Who didn't? What did you wish someone had said or done?
- What parts of the job follow you home most often: irritability, numbness, sleep disruption, scanning, or disconnection?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
