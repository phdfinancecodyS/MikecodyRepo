# Shame and Intimacy Repair: Pattern Finder

**Audience:** First Responder

**From guide:** Shame and Intimacy Repair

---

Goal: Spot the shame-intimacy pattern: when it shows up, what triggers it, and what your body does.

Prompts:
- What happens in your body when intimacy gets close: do you freeze, pull away, shut down, or get angry?
- What does shame tell you about yourself in those moments? Write it word for word.
- When's the last time you felt genuinely safe being seen by your partner? What was different?
- What's the earliest memory of learning that being seen was dangerous?
- What does your partner see when shame takes over: and what do they not see?
- What parts of the job follow you home most often: irritability, numbness, sleep disruption, scanning, or disconnection?

---

**A quick note:** This worksheet is educational: it's not therapy, it's not a diagnosis, and using it doesn't create a clinical relationship between us. It's built to help you take the next step, not replace professional support. If you're in crisis, contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line), or call 911.
