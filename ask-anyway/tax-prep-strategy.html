💡 Tax-Saving Tips for 2025
🏦
Max Your IRA Contribution
You can contribute up to $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+) to a Traditional IRA by April 15 and deduct it from 2025 income.
🏥
Use Your HSA
HSA contributions ($4,150 individual / $8,300 family) are triple-tax advantaged — deductible, grow tax-free, and withdrawals for medical are tax-free.
📉
Tax-Loss Harvesting
Investment losses offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar. Up to $3,000 excess losses can offset ordinary income each year.
🎁
Bunching Charitable Donations
If itemizing is marginal, consider donating 2 years of contributions in one year to exceed the standard deduction threshold.
🏠
Home Office Deduction
Self-employed? The simplified method allows $5/sq ft (up to 300 sq ft) or you can calculate actual expenses for the dedicated home office space.
🚗
Business Mileage
The 2025 standard mileage rate is 70¢/mile for business use. Keep a mileage log throughout the year for best results.
⏰
File an Extension if Needed
Form 4868 gives you until Oct 15, 2026 to FILE, but you must PAY any taxes owed by April 15 to avoid penalties.
🔄
Roth vs Traditional IRA
If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be better despite no current deduction — contributions grow tax-free.
👶
Child Tax Credit
Up to $2,000 per qualifying child under 17. Phase-out starts at $200K (single) / $400K (MFJ). Partially refundable up to $1,700.
