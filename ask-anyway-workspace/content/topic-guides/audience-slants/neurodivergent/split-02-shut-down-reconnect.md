## What This Helps With

You went quiet. Checked out. Shut the door. And now you don't know how to come back. Here's how to reconnect without it being a whole thing.

This version is written with neurodivergent life in mind, including overwhelm, sensory load, executive function strain, rejection sensitivity, masking, and burnout.

## What Is Happening

A lot of mental health advice quietly assumes a nervous system and executive function style that you may not have. If you're neurodivergent, the same problem can hit harder because of sensory overload, task switching, time blindness, social effort, or the cost of masking. That doesn't mean you're doing it wrong. It means the plan has to fit your brain.

This pattern usually starts in your body before it shows up in your words.
When intensity jumps fast, your best move is early interruption, not long explanation.
Repair is part of the plan, not a side note.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this problem is being intensified by sensory overload, masking, executive friction, or the cost of trying to function in a way that doesn't fit you.
2. Catch the earliest body cue and call a short reset.
3. Use one grounding action for 60-90 seconds.
4. Return with one repair sentence and one clear next step.

## What To Say

- I need a plan that fits how my brain actually works, not advice built for someone else’s system.
- Some of this isn't laziness or avoidance. It's executive and sensory load.
- I'm getting overloaded and I don't want to make this worse.
- Give me a short reset and I'll come back ready to continue.
- I care about this conversation, and I'm ready to restart better.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Using shame to force yourself into systems, routines, or expectations that are already failing your nervous system.
- Explaining behavior before owning impact.
- Taking distance with no return time.
- Skipping repair because things feel awkward.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: notice that you've checked out and name it. "I'm shut down right now."
- Before bed tonight: text one person and say: "I went quiet. I'm still here."
- Tomorrow: re-enter one conversation or relationship you pulled away from. You don't need a reason — just show back up.
- Brain-friendly support step: use a structure that works with how your brain actually operates — a visual reminder, a body double, a voice memo, or a low-barrier check-in that doesn't require sitting down and writing.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Shut Down Reconnect" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
