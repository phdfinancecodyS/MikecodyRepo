## What This Helps With

You're trying to be a good parent while running on fumes. If you feel like you're failing at the thing that matters most, this guide is honest about what helps.

This version is written for people who want practical mental health help that can sit alongside Christian faith, prayer, and church life without turning into a sermon.

## What Is Happening

When your faith matters to you, pain often hits two places at once: your nervous system and your beliefs about God, hope, suffering, and support. The goal here isn't to over-spiritualize the problem or ignore faith. It's to tell the truth about what's happening and use support that fits your real life.

Parenting on empty means you're out of fuel. Patience is gone. Presence is impossible. You're in survival mode. Your kids read your absence and they act out more because they feel the disconnection. You feel guilt about not being the parent you want to be.

## What To Do Now

1. Name what's a mental health problem, what's a faith question, and where the two are overlapping so you don't blur them into one knot.
2. Pick one non-negotiable parenting anchor (bedtime, one meal together, morning check-in) and protect only that.
3. Release guilt about everything else. You can't do all things when you're depleted.
4. Get help with the non-anchors (school pickup, supper prep, bedtime on your off-nights).

## What To Say

- I'm struggling, and I don't need a polished answer right now. I need honest support.
- Prayer can be part of this, and I also need practical help.
- I'm running on empty and I can only show up for [one specific thing].
- I need help with [specific tasks] so I can protect my presence for what matters most.
- When I come back to capacity, I'll be more available. Right now, this is what I can do.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Using spiritual language to avoid the actual problem, the actual action step, or the actual support you need.
- Trying to fix the whole relationship in one conversation.
- Having the conversation when neither person is regulated enough for it.
- Skipping clear repair, boundaries, or follow-up after the hard moment.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: give yourself one 15-minute break today. Not when the kids are asleep — before that. Let something be messy.
- Before bed tonight: name one thing you did right as a parent today, even if it was small.
- Tomorrow: ask for one specific help from one specific person. Not "I need help" — "can you take them for an hour?"
- Faith support step: take this to prayer or bring it to one trusted person in your faith community — you don't have to carry this privately when you've a whole community and a God you can bring it to.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Parenting on Empty" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
