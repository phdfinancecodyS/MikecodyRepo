## What This Helps With

You keep doing the thing you said you'd stop. Not because you're weak — because the loop is wired. Here's how to rewire it, one cycle at a time.

This version is written for educators dealing with emotional labor, performance pressure, burnout, care overload, and the reality of trying to keep showing up while running low.

## What Is Happening

Education work asks for constant emotional output, patience, attention, and management of other people’s needs. That can make mental health problems feel invisible because you're still performing. But being able to teach or lead a room doesn't mean you're okay.

Habit loops feel automatic because cue, relief, and repeat happen fast.
The break point is usually right after the trigger, before the old routine starts.
Replacement routines work better than pure willpower.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask how much of this pattern is tied to the emotional labor of always being on, available, and responsible for the tone of the room.
2. Name the cue and where it usually hits.
3. Insert one replacement action for 5-10 minutes.
4. Track one win and one miss without shame.

## What To Say

- I can still do the job and still be struggling.
- I need support that accounts for emotional labor, not just workload.
- I can feel the old loop starting, so I'm switching routines now.
- I'm choosing a smaller, safer action for the next ten minutes.
- Progress is reps, not perfection.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Using your ability to stay functional in front of others as proof that nothing needs attention.
- Trying to quit a loop with no replacement routine.
- Using shame as motivation.
- Treating one slip like total failure.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: identify the cue that starts the loop. What happens right before the habit kicks in?
- Before bed tonight: write down: cue → routine → reward. Once you see the loop, you can break it.
- Tomorrow: when the cue hits, replace the routine with one different action. Same cue, new response.
- Boundary support step: use one habit that genuinely separates your teacher identity from your recovery space today — no lesson plans, no parent emails, no student problems — even for 30 minutes.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Habit Loop Reset" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
