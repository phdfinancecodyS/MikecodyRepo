## What This Helps With

You're tired in a way that sleep doesn't fix. If you've been running on empty so long it feels normal, this is the guide that meets you there.

This version is written for people carrying grief, fresh loss, old loss that's still active, or the kind of missing that changes how every other problem lands.

## What Is Happening

Grief changes the shape of everything. It lowers capacity, intensifies loneliness, scrambles concentration, and can make normal stress feel enormous. It also doesn't move in a straight line. The goal isn't to rush you past grief. It's to help you function while grief is still part of the room.

Chronic sleep debt isn't about missing sleep one night. It's the accumulated exhaustion of weeks or months of poor sleep. Your body can't catch up with random long sleeps. Your brain is degraded. Your mood is wrecked. Recovery takes consistent repair, not heroic one-time catches.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask how much of this problem is being amplified by grief load, anniversaries, reminders, or the effort of trying to function while carrying absence.
2. Pick one anchor point (7am wake or 11pm sleep) and protect it ruthlessly for 2 weeks.
3. Use the same wind-down 30 minutes before bed every night (no negotiating).
4. In week 2, add one more anchor point. Don't try to fix everything at once.

## What To Say

- This problem isn't happening separate from grief. Grief is part of the load right now.
- I don't need to be over this to take a useful next step.
- My sleep debt is real and catching up takes time, not one long sleep.
- I'm protecting my sleep anchors because that's how I get out of this.
- I'm going to feel worse before I feel better, and that's part of recovery.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Judging yourself for reduced capacity without accounting for the real weight of loss.
- Trying a completely different sleep fix every night.
- Using screens, scrolling, or stimulation as your wind-down plan.
- Expecting one good night to repair long-term sleep strain.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: set a hard stop time for screens tonight — one hour before you want to be asleep.
- Before bed tonight: do one boring thing for 15 minutes (fold laundry, read something dull). Let your brain wind down.
- Tomorrow: move your wake-up time 15 minutes earlier and resist the snooze. Start small.
- Grief support step: let yourself have the moment without needing it to be done or resolved — then identify one small act of care you can offer yourself before the day ends.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Chronic Sleep Debt" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
