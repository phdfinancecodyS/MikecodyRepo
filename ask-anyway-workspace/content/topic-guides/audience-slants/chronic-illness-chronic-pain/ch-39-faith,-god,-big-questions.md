## What This Helps With

When you're asking 'where was God in that?' or your faith doesn't hold what it used to — this guide sits with those questions instead of rushing past them.

This version is written for people whose mental health load is tangled with symptoms, flare cycles, pain, fatigue, uncertainty, and the extra work of living in a body that takes more management.

## What Is Happening

Chronic illness and chronic pain change the rules. Energy is less predictable, symptoms can wipe out capacity fast, and a lot of advice doesn't account for the real cost of simply getting through a day. Your mental health is affected by both the condition and the life restriction around it.

Faith, God, and big questions become huge when you've seen things that violate your belief system. Why does God allow suffering? How do I reconcile faith with what I've seen? Is there meaning? Is there purpose? These aren't abstract. They're about whether your life means anything.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask where this problem gets sharper during flares, fatigue, pain spikes, or the frustration of not being able to do what you want at the pace you want.
2. Sit with the questions without needing to answer them right now.
3. Separate faith from theology: You can have faith questions without needing doctrine answers.
4. Find community with others asking the same questions (not people with final answers, but people in the question with you).

## What To Say

- My body is part of this problem, not just the backdrop.
- I need a plan that respects energy limits and symptom reality.
- My faith is being shaken and I'm sitting in the questions.
- I don't have answers about meaning and purpose right now and that's okay.
- I'm finding community with others who are asking these questions too.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Setting expectations that ignore pain, fatigue, flare cycles, or the recovery cost of everyday tasks.
- Treating shame like proof instead of one part of the story.
- Isolating with guilt instead of saying the truth out loud somewhere safe.
- Trying to rush forgiveness before honest accountability and repair.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: say the question you've been afraid to ask out loud. Even if it's angry. Especially if it's angry.
- Before bed tonight: sit with the question for five minutes without trying to answer it.
- Tomorrow: find one person — a friend, a spiritual leader, a counselor — and tell them where your faith is right now.
- Energy-aware support step: plan one support action that accounts for your actual capacity today — not what you could do on a good day — and give yourself permission to count small as real.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Faith, God, Big Questions" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
