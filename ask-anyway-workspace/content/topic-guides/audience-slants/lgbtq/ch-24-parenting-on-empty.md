## What This Helps With

You're trying to be a good parent while running on fumes. If you feel like you're failing at the thing that matters most, this guide is honest about what helps.

This version is written with LGBTQ+ life in mind, including identity stress, belonging, safety, chosen family, and the extra labor of figuring out where you can fully exhale.

## What Is Happening

Mental health problems don't land in a vacuum. If you're LGBTQ+, the issue may be sitting on top of identity stress, rejection history, minority stress, hiding, code-switching, or exhaustion from always reading whether a space is safe. That doesn't mean your identity is the problem. It means the context matters.

Parenting on empty means you're out of fuel. Patience is gone. Presence is impossible. You're in survival mode. Your kids read your absence and they act out more because they feel the disconnection. You feel guilt about not being the parent you want to be.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether this problem is being made worse by unsafety, hiding, isolation, or being around people who don't actually let you be known.
2. Pick one non-negotiable parenting anchor (bedtime, one meal together, morning check-in) and protect only that.
3. Release guilt about everything else. You can't do all things when you're depleted.
4. Get help with the non-anchors (school pickup, supper prep, bedtime on your off-nights).

## What To Say

- I need support that doesn't make me shrink, explain myself, or scan the room first.
- Part of what makes this harder is the environment, not just me.
- I'm running on empty and I can only show up for [one specific thing].
- I need help with [specific tasks] so I can protect my presence for what matters most.
- When I come back to capacity, I'll be more available. Right now, this is what I can do.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Treating identity stress like it's irrelevant background noise when it's actively shaping the problem.
- Trying to fix the whole relationship in one conversation.
- Having the conversation when neither person is regulated enough for it.
- Skipping clear repair, boundaries, or follow-up after the hard moment.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: give yourself one 15-minute break today. Not when the kids are asleep — before that. Let something be messy.
- Before bed tonight: name one thing you did right as a parent today, even if it was small.
- Tomorrow: ask for one specific help from one specific person. Not "I need help" — "can you take them for an hour?"
- Affirming support step: connect with one person or space where you don't have to justify or explain yourself before you can be supported — chosen family, a community group, or one person who already gets it.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Parenting on Empty" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
