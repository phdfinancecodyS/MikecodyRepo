## What This Helps With

You learned to do everything alone. It kept you alive. But it's costing you now. This guide is about letting people back in without losing yourself.

This version is written for healthcare workers who carry clinical pressure, moral stress, compassion fatigue, shift strain, and the weird tension of caring for everybody while neglecting yourself.

## What Is Happening

Healthcare work teaches you to override your own body, emotions, and limits in order to care for other people. That can become a problem fast. You may be good at triaging everyone else while ignoring what's happening in you until it spills over.

Lone wolf to team member is learning to trust a team when your self-reliance has been your survival mechanism. Being part of something means vulnerability. It means depending on others. Your hypervigilance makes team trust feel impossible. But you're learning that operation is better with team.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether you're applying professional competence to everyone except yourself, and what it's costing you.
2. Start with one person on the team. Practice vulnerability in small doses (ask for help, admit you don't know, say you struggled).
3. Notice what happens: The team usually responds with stronger connection, not judgment.
4. Gradually expand: More people, more vulnerability, more trust.

## What To Say

- I know how to care for people. I'm not doing a good job of applying that same honesty to myself right now.
- This isn't just stress. It's accumulation, moral strain, and not enough recovery.
- I'm a lone wolf and team trust doesn't come easy to me.
- I'm practicing asking for help and admitting when I'm struggling.
- I'm learning that depending on a team doesn't make me weak.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Using clinical knowledge to explain your own problem while still avoiding the practical steps you would recommend to someone else.
- Treating work strain like it should stay neatly separated from the rest of your life.
- Building a plan that only works on calm weeks instead of real ones.
- Ignoring identity loss, overload, or purpose strain because you're still functioning.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: think about the last time someone offered help and you said no. What would've happened if you'd said yes?
- Before bed tonight: name one thing you're carrying alone that you don't actually have to.
- Tomorrow: ask one person for one specific thing. "Can you handle this today?" — and let them.
- Boundary support step: use one hard boundary between patient care and your own recovery space today — even imperfect counts, and your health isn't less important than your patients'.

## Worksheet 1: Pattern Finder

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
- When did "Lone Wolf to Healthy Team Member" last show up for you? What was the situation?
- What did you notice first — in your body, your thoughts, or your behavior?
- What makes this pattern worse? What conditions or stressors fuel it?
- What have you tried before, and what actually helped (even a little)?
- Who in your life knows about this? Who doesn't — and why?
