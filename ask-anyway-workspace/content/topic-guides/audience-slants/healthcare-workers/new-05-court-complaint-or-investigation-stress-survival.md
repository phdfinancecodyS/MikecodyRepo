## What This Helps With

You're under investigation, facing a complaint, or headed to court. The stress is different from anything else. Here's how to survive the process.

This version is written for healthcare workers who carry clinical pressure, moral stress, compassion fatigue, shift strain, and the weird tension of caring for everybody while neglecting yourself.

## What Is Happening

Healthcare work teaches you to override your own body, emotions, and limits in order to care for other people. That can become a problem fast. You may be good at triaging everyone else while ignoring what's happening in you until it spills over.

An investigation or complaint against you triggers threat detection in your body—your job status is threatened, your reputation is on the line, and you're probably scared. This isn't time for trying to feel normal. This is time for protecting yourself, following your union or legal guidance exactly, and managing the actual crisis, not your feelings about it.

## What To Do Now

1. Ask whether you're applying professional competence to everyone except yourself, and what it's costing you.
2. Get legal representation or union help immediately—don't skip this step.
3. Follow their guidance exactly. Don't try to handle this alone.
4. Set a time limit for daily worry (say, 20 minutes) and discipline yourself to it.

## What To Say

- I know how to care for people. I'm not doing a good job of applying that same honesty to myself right now.
- This isn't just stress. It's accumulation, moral strain, and not enough recovery.
- I'm in an investigation and I need my representative at every step.
- I'm not answering questions without guidance because I'm protecting myself appropriately.
- This is temporary and stressful and I'm doing the right things.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Using clinical knowledge to explain your own problem while still avoiding the practical steps you would recommend to someone else.
- Treating every problem like it must be solved tonight.
- Avoiding the issue until it gets bigger.
- Trying to carry this alone without one support check-in.

## 24-Hour Action Plan

- Right now: separate today from the outcome. You can't control the result. You can control how you get through today.
- Before bed tonight: write down what you're most afraid of. Getting it out of your head takes power away from it.
- Tomorrow: talk to your rep, your attorney, or a trusted supporter. Don't carry the legal stress alone.
- Boundary support step: use one hard boundary between patient care and your own recovery space today — even imperfect counts, and your health isn't less important than your patients'.

Goal: Spot the pattern — when it shows up, what triggers it, and what you notice first.

Prompts:
